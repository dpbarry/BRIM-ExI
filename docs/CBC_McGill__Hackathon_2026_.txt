	ABOUT US
The Claude Builder Club (CBC) is McGill University's premier community for AI fluency and responsible AI development, established in partnership with Anthropic
Our Mission
We teach students the difference between true AI engineering and "AI slop." Through workshops, hackathons, and hands-on projects, we emphasize responsible AI usage where engineers maintain ownership of their work through proper feedback loops and domain expertise.

HACKATHON THEME: SOCIAL IMPACT
Inspired by Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei's essay Machines of Loving Grace, this hackathon asks one question: how can AI help people flourish?
We're not looking for solutions to hypothetical problems. We want tools built for specific people facing real challenges. We want to see your thinking as much as your code.
Five tracks:
1. Biology & Physical Health. Healthcare is expensive, inaccessible, and confusing. Build something that helps people access care, understand their health, or navigate complex medical systems.
Examples: 
* Diagnostic aids for underserved clinics
* Patient education platforms 
* Medication adherence tools
* Symptom assessment and triage 
* Health literacy for treatment decisions 
* Accessibility tools for people with disabilities navigating healthcare
Track-specific ethical considerations:
* Medical accuracy - when should this hand off to a real professional?
* Privacy of deeply sensitive health data
* Bias in medical AI across different populations
* Transparency about limitations and when professional care is needed
2. Neuroscience & Mental Health. There aren't enough therapists. Stigma keeps people from seeking help. Geography and insurance gate good care. Build tools that expand access to mental health support and understanding.
Examples: 
* Always-available therapeutic support using evidence-based approaches  
* Mental health literacy and psychoeducation 
* Tools for recognizing when you need professional help 
* Stress and burnout prevention 
* Peer support facilitation 
* Accessibility tools for neurodivergent individuals
Track-specific ethical considerations:
* Crisis situations - when does someone need immediate human intervention?
* Privacy and stigma around mental health data
* Avoiding harm through bad psychological advice
* Cultural competence in mental health approaches
* Transparency about what AI therapy can and can't do
* Not replacing human connection when it's needed


3. Economic Empowerment & Education. Talent is everywhere. Opportunity isn't. Remove barriers to learning, skill-building, and economic advancement for people currently locked out.
Examples: 
* Adaptive tutoring  
* Career transition guidance  
* Financial literacy for underserved communities 
* Job interview prep 
* Small business/nonprofit support 
* Scholarship matching 
* Practical language learning
Track-specific ethical considerations:
* Who gets excluded by your design (tech requirements, literacy levels, language)?
* Impact of bad advice when guiding careers or education
* Empowering informed choices vs. making choices for people
* Cultural assumptions about what "success" means


4. Governance & Collaboration. Democratic institutions are strained. Civic participation is falling. Build something that helps people engage, deliberate, and make collective decisions.
(Track 4 has been removed)
5. Creative Flourishing. As AI handles routine work, questions of human purpose grow urgent. Amplify creativity. Preserve culture. Help people find meaning.
Every team must answer three questions:
* Who are you building this for, and why do they need it?
* What could go wrong, and what would you do about it?
* How does this help people rather than make decisions for them?
Examples: 
* Community storytelling platforms
* Music/art education with real-time feedback  
* Writing development tools 
* Language preservation 
* Skill development for crafts/instruments 
* Intergenerational knowledge exchange 
* Purpose exploration guides
Track-specific ethical considerations:
* Keeping humans as creators using tools (not AI as creator with human prompter)
* Enhancing vs. short-cutting the creative process in ways that diminish it
* Cultural appropriation vs. respectful celebration
* Homogenization of creative voices
* Creating vs. destroying value for creative workers


Three sub-challenges: 
1. Rootly Sub-Challenge: Engineer Wellbeing
About Rootly
Rootly builds incident management software for engineering teams. When production breaks, Rootly helps teams respond fast, coordinate across tools, and learn from what went wrong. 
The Problem
On-call rotations are a hidden health risk. Engineers get paged at 2 AM, work through weekends, and absorb stress that never shows up in a Jira ticket. Rootly built On-Call Health to measure this burnout risk: tracking workload, alert volume, and shift patterns to surface who is at risk before they burn out.
The Challenge
You can do either option 1 or 2 or both:
1. Build a project that uses at least one of these tools to address a real problem in engineer health, incident response, or team operations:
* Rootly MCP Server — 99 tools covering incidents, schedules, escalation policies, on-call shifts, and more. Connect it to Claude to resolve incidents, generate handoff summaries, or analyze team patterns.
* MCP On-Call Health — An MCP server for the On-Call Health API. Analyze burnout risk, query workload data, and surface at-risk responders directly from your AI assistant.
* Rootly CLI — Command-line access to the Rootly API. Build scripts, automations, or developer tools on top of it.
2. Build on top of Rootly (their main product). If you proceed with this option, please let us know on discord, we will provide you Rootly credentials. 
This sub-challenge sits within the Neuroscience & Mental Health track. Your project can also qualify for the main track prize.
2. PCare+ Sub-Challenge: 
* Best Project in Bio and Physical Health
3. Brim Financial Sub-Challenge: 
You can do either option 1 or 2:
1. AI-Powered Expense Intelligence for SMBs
2. Cross-Border Payment System Design
For Brim, we pick the best of each (there will be 2 winning projects). Either way, your project can also qualify for the main prize.

HOW TO INTEGRATE CLAUDE CODE
Option 1: Claude Code Workflows. Use Claude Code as your AI pair programmer in the terminal. Push to GitHub and tag Claude AI as a collaborator. For example: Co-Authored-By: Claude Sonnet 4.6 <noreply@anthropic.com> Show how you maintained ownership through code review and architectural decisions.
Option 2: Claude API Integration. Build applications using Claude's API. Implement conversational AI, document analysis, code generation, or content creation. Demonstrate proper prompt engineering, error handling, and rate limiting. McGill students get free 25$ in API credits by attending one of the workshops, please show up 1 week before the hackathon to claim it.
Option 3: Hybrid. Use Claude Code to build your infrastructure and Claude API for runtime, user-facing features. AI pair programming plus AI-powered functionality.
What we don't want:
* Projects you can't explain technically
* Shallow wrappers around Claude API calls with no added value
* Harmful applications or ethical violations
* Copy-paste solutions where you didn't maintain engineering ownership

JUDGING CRITERIA
Projects will be evaluated on four key dimensions::
Real-World Impact
25%
	• Does this solve a real problem? Who benefits and how? Could it scale?
	Technical Execution
30%
	• Does it work? Is it well-built? Does it use AI effectively?
	Ethical Alignment
25%
	• Does it center human dignity? Does it address potential harms? Does it expand access equitably?
	Presentation Quality
20%
	•Can you clearly explain what you built and why it matters?
	⚠️ CRITICAL: You MUST be able to explain your technical stack and architectural decisions. Using AI coding tools is encouraged, but you own the project, not the AI. Some judges will ask technical questions.
